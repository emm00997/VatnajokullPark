About The Park

Vatnajokull National Park is located in the eastern through southeastern part of Iceland. The park encompasses all of Europe's largest glacier Vatnajokull. The park covers an area of 5,459.9 square miles (14,141 sq km)
accounting for about 14% of Iceland.
It's is so big that it can be separated into four different sections: The Northern Territory, Southern Territory,
Western Territory and Eastern Territory.
All with a vast variety of different Activities, Sights, Settings, Adventures,Nature and More!


The Glacier

Vatnajokull Glacier is renowned around the world
The glacier covers more than 8% of the country measuring at over 8.100 km2 (3.127 sq mi).
The ice cap of Vatnajokull Glacier serves as the source for 30 different outlet glaciers,
There are many different ways to access parts of the Vatnajokull Glacier, but the most common are:
Skaftafell, Jokulsarlon Glacier Lagoon, and Jokulsargljufur in the north, which you can visit. year-round.
But even-though you can visit these spots all year round it’s highly advised to go with a tour guide.


The North

In the Northern Territory of Vatnajokull
Two of the most prominent sights many people come to see are the horseshoe shaped cliffs Asbygri,
Which were formed by enormous catastrophic glacial bursts that carved out the deep ravines and rocky basins
The most famous of which is the horseshoe-shaped canyon Asbygri. Nearby the Asbyrgi is Europe's most powerful waterfall, The Dettifoss. With a drop of 44 m and a flow of 200 cubic meters per second plummeting over the edge,
Dettifoss is fed by the powerful glacier river Jokulsa a Fjollum which flows from The largest glacier in Europe, Vatnajokull.


The South

The Vatnajokull Ice Cave
Also known as Anaconda Ice Cave and Crystal Ice Cave. The glacier gets its “crystal” name because of its crystal-blue ice.
The cave can be found on the south side of the Vatnajokull glacier and the ice looks blue due to the density of the it. The ice is much thicker and denser than regular ice for example, an ice cube in your freezer takes only a few hours to freeze where as glacier ice is a result of a process that takes hundreds of years of snowflakes falling and compressing and re-crystallizing into ice, creating the crystal clear blue ice. The best time to see the caves and get a tour is,
Early November - Late April.


Snaefell Wilderness Area

Closeby to the Ice caves is The Snaefell Wilderness Area
Snæfell is the name of the tallest mountain in Iceland that is currently not underneath a glacier cap.
The mountain and the plains surrounding it are famously known as the grazing grounds for the Icelandic population of reindeer.
A large amount of undiscovered hiking trails make their way through the area, taking you far off the well-beaten Icelandic path to discover your own small part of the country.
Aside from sighting the herds of reindeer, you’ll find all of the usual natural sights on offer as well: Waterfalls, Hot Springs, and Great views of glaciers glistening in the distance.


The West

Langisjor is among the clearest highland lakes in the country.
Langisjor itself is located 662 meters above sea level and is approximately 20 km long and up to 2 km wide.
The lake can plunge down 75 meters to the lake bed in certain spots.
Langisjor Lake was formed by volcanic faults collapsing into the ground causing the lake itself to look like it's trapped between two ridges, Pushing the water that flows through it coming from the nearby glaciers into the middle. One of the best places to admire this lake is from the summit of Svestindur
The view from up there is unlike anything you've ever seen.


The East

Deep in the northeast region of the Icelandic Highland
You will find a remote and fascinating place called Kverkfjoll. Located north of Vatnajokull ice cap, Kverkfjoll is a captivating site that reveals the combination of ice and magma and how both forces constantly work together in shaping Iceland. Kverkfjoll is home to Iceland's largest geothermal area: Warm geothermal pools, Chilling ice caves,
Challenging hiking paths and a harsh landscape between glacier tongues Bruaarjokull and Dyngjujokull, which regularly exhibit signs of possible eruptions.
Kverkfjoll is harsh and highly unpredictable.
It is always advised to go with a guide when touring these breathtaking sights.
